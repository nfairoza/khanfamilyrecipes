Palak Ulikaram

from the kitchen of Noor

A big bowl for the family · a tangy afternoon dish

What you'll need

For the ground masala

  • 1 big onion, roughly chopped
  • a fistful of garlic cloves
  • a lime-sized ball of tamarind
  • a good handful of dried red chilies
  • 2–3 spoons coriander seeds (dhania)
  • a spoon of cumin (zira)
  • salt, to taste

For the tempering & greens

  • oil, a generous pour
  • mustard seeds & cumin (zira)
  • chana dal & urad dal, a spoon each
  • a sprig of curry leaves
  • a pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • red chili powder, to your bravery
  • turmeric (haldi), a good pinch
  • lots & lots of spinach, chopped
  • coriander leaves, to finish
  • ghee, for serving

Amounts are by feel — start with more garlic and chili than seems wise, then taste as you go.

How we make it

  1. Roast the whole spices. Lightly dry-sauté the dried red chilies, coriander seeds and cumin until warm and toasty — don't burn them.
  2. Grind. Blend the onion, garlic, tamarind, salt and the roasted spices to a coarse paste.
  3. Temper. Heat oil; add mustard seeds, cumin, chana dal, urad dal and curry leaves. Let it splutter and the dals turn golden; add a pinch of hing.
  4. Fry the paste. Add the ground masala and fry a few minutes until it darkens and the oil separates.
  5. Spice it. Add chili powder, turmeric and a little more salt if needed.
  6. Cook the spinach. Add lots of chopped spinach. Cook and cook until the water is gone and the oil separates again.
  7. Finish. Stir through coriander leaves. Serve with hot rice and ghee.
Amma's note: the whole secret is patience at the end — keep cooking until the oil comes back. And add more garlic than you think.
