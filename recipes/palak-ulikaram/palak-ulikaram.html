A big bowl for the family · a lazy, tangy afternoon dish

Spinach cooked down with a tangy, garlicky Andhra masala — roasted chilies and coriander ground with tamarind and a whole lot of garlic, then simmered into the greens until the oil comes shining back to the top. Eat it with hot white rice and a spoon of ghee, and don't count the garlic cloves.

What you'll need

For the ground masala

  • 1 big onion, roughly chopped
  • a whole lot of garlic — a fistful of cloves
  • a lime-sized ball of tamarind
  • a good handful of dried red chilies
  • 2–3 spoons whole coriander seeds (dhania)
  • a spoon of cumin (zira)
  • salt, to taste

For the tempering & greens

  • oil, a generous pour
  • mustard seeds & cumin (zira)
  • chana dal & urad dal, a spoon each
  • a sprig of curry leaves
  • a pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • red chili powder, to your bravery
  • turmeric (haldi), a good pinch
  • lots & lots of spinach, finely chopped
  • chopped coriander leaves, to finish
  • ghee, for serving

Amounts are by feel — start with more garlic and chili than seems wise, then taste as you go.

How we make it

  1. Roast the whole spices. Lightly dry-sauté the dried red chilies, coriander seeds and cumin in a pan until they smell warm and toasty — just fragrant, don't burn them.
  2. Grind the masala. Into the blender goes the onion, all that garlic, the tamarind, salt and your roasted chilies, coriander and cumin. Blend to a coarse paste — some texture is good.
  3. Start the tempering. Heat a generous pour of oil until hot. Add mustard seeds, cumin, chana dal, urad dal and the curry leaves — let it splutter and the dals turn golden. Add a pinch of asafoetida.
  4. Fry the paste. Tip in the ground masala and fry for a few minutes, stirring, until it darkens and the oil separates out at the edges.
  5. Spice it. Add red chili powder, turmeric and a little more salt if it needs it. Give it a stir.
  6. Cook down the spinach. Now add lots and lots of chopped spinach. Cook it and cook it and cook it — until all the water has gone and the oil separates again. That's how you know.
  7. Finish. Stir through chopped coriander leaves. Serve with hot white rice and a spoon of ghee.
Amma's note: the whole secret is patience at the end — keep cooking the spinach until the oil comes back. Rushing it leaves it watery. And really, add more garlic than you think.
