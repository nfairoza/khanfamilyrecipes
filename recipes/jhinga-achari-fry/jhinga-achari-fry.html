Jhinga Achari Fry

Prawns marinated in a pickle-spiced masala, then fried till they catch at the edges. The spoonful of achar — mango, tomato, whatever's open in the fridge — is the trick: it gives the whole thing that tangy, pickly kick. Wonderful with rice and dal, or just rice and a bit of chutney.

Jhinga Achari Fry

from the kitchen of Noor

A plateful of prawns · quick fry, big flavour

What you'll need

Prawns & marinade

  • prawns (jhinga), heads & tails off, washed
  • ginger-garlic paste, a good spoon
  • turmeric (haldi), a pinch
  • red chili powder (mirchi), to taste
  • salt (namak), to taste
  • coriander powder (dhania) — a little extra
  • cumin powder (jeera)
  • kalonji (black seeds)
  • cumin seeds (zira)
  • a spoon of achar — mango, tomato or mixed-veg

Frying & finishing

  • oil, for a hot shallow fry
  • a sprig of curry leaves
  • chopped coriander leaves, to garnish

Any pickle works — the achar is what makes it "achari." Start with a small spoon and taste.

How we make it

  1. Prep the prawns. Remove the heads and tails, then wash well and drain.
  2. Marinate. Add ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, chili powder, salt, extra coriander powder and cumin powder. Then the kalonji and cumin seeds.
  3. The achari touch. Stir in a spoon of achar. Mix well and let it marinate a while.
  4. Heat the oil. Get the oil hot and drop in the curry leaves to crackle.
  5. Fry the prawns. Lay them in one by one and fry, turning, until cooked through and catching at the edges.
  6. Finish. Garnish with coriander leaves. Serve hot with rice and dal, or rice and chutney.
Amma's note: don't crowd the pan — lay the prawns in one by one so they fry, not steam. And don't overcook; prawns are done the moment they curl and turn opaque.
