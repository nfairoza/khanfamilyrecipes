Jhinga Achari Fry

from the kitchen of Noor

A plateful of prawns · quick fry, big flavour

Prawns marinated in a pickle-spiced masala, then fried till they catch at the edges. The spoonful of achar — mango, tomato, whatever's open in the fridge — is the trick: it gives the whole thing that tangy, pickly kick. Wonderful with rice and dal, or just rice and a bit of chutney.

What you'll need

For the prawns & marinade

  • prawns (jhinga), heads & tails removed, washed
  • ginger-garlic paste, a good spoon
  • turmeric (haldi), a pinch
  • red chili powder (mirchi), to taste
  • salt (namak), to taste
  • coriander powder (dhania) — a little extra
  • cumin powder (jeera)
  • kalonji (black seeds)
  • cumin seeds (zira)
  • a spoon of achar — mango, tomato or mixed-veg pickle

For frying & finishing

  • oil, for a hot shallow fry
  • a sprig of curry leaves
  • chopped coriander leaves, to garnish

Any pickle you have works — the achar is what makes it "achari." Start with a small spoon and taste.

How we make it

  1. Prep the prawns. Remove the heads and tails, then wash the prawns well and drain.
  2. Marinate. Add ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, chili powder, salt, a little extra coriander powder and cumin powder. Then add the kalonji (black seeds) and cumin seeds.
  3. The achari touch. Stir in a spoon of whatever achar you have — mango, tomato or mixed-veg pickle. Mix well and let it marinate a while.
  4. Heat the oil. Get the oil hot in a pan and drop in the curry leaves to let them crackle.
  5. Fry the prawns. Lay the prawns in one by one and fry — turning — until they're cooked through and catching at the edges.
  6. Finish. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with rice and dal, or rice and chutney.
Amma's note: don't crowd the pan — lay the prawns in one by one so they fry, not steam. And don't overcook them; prawns are done the moment they curl and turn opaque.
