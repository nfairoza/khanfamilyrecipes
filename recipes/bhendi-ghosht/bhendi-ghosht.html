Bhendi Ghosht

from the kitchen of Noor

A hearty pot for the family · pressure-cooked, then finished

What you'll need

  • mutton (goat), cut into pieces
  • bhendi (okra / ladies finger), trimmed
  • onions, chopped
  • tomatoes, chopped
  • ginger-garlic paste, a good spoon
  • whole spices — cloves (long) & cinnamon
  • oil, a generous pour
  • turmeric (haldi), a pinch
  • red chili powder (mirchi), to taste
  • coriander powder (dhania)
  • salt (namak), to taste
  • tamarind — paste, or concentrate in water
  • garam masala powder
  • curry leaves · coriander leaves · green chilies

Amounts by feel. Pressure-cook time depends on the meat: ~22 min for smallish pieces, ~35 min for larger ones.

How we make it

  1. Fry the onions. Heat oil and fry the chopped onions until golden brown.
  2. Whole spices. Add the cloves and cinnamon, then the ginger-garlic paste; fry until the raw smell is gone.
  3. Tomatoes. Add the chopped tomatoes and fry until softened and pulpy.
  4. Brown the mutton. Add the mutton pieces and fry a little. Add turmeric, chili powder, salt, then coriander powder.
  5. Add tamarind & water. Add a little water plus tamarind paste (or tamarind concentrate in water). Stir.
  6. Pressure-cook. Cook the meat until tender — ~22 min for small pieces, ~35 min for larger.
  7. Add the bhendi. Stir in the okra with a sprinkle of garam masala, curry leaves, coriander leaves and green chilies.
  8. Finish. Cook until the bhendi is tender (or pressure-cook 5–10 min more). Serve hot with rice.
Amma's note: add the bhendi only after the meat is cooked so it keeps its shape and doesn't go slimy. The tamarind is what makes it tangy — start small and adjust.
