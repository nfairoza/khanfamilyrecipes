Aloo Ghosht White Curry

No turmeric, no red chili — just green chilies and whole spices. A milder curry, but deeper and richer for it, with soft potatoes that soak up all the juices. The heat comes from green chilies, the body from yogurt, and the fragrance from a coarse-crushed spice mix stirred in at the end. You'll love the potatoes in it.

Aloo Ghosht White Curry

from the kitchen of Noor

A hearty pot · pressure-cooked, mild & rich

What you'll need

The curry

  • mutton (ghosht), cut into pieces
  • potatoes, peeled & cut
  • onions, thinly sliced
  • ginger-garlic paste, a good spoon
  • lots of green chilies
  • plain yogurt (dahi), about a cup
  • a handful of mint leaves
  • olive oil · salt (all of it, up front)
  • whole spices — cloves, cinnamon, cardamom, bay leaves

The coarse spice mix

  • coriander seeds (dhania)
  • cumin seeds (jeera)
  • black peppercorns (kali mirch)
  • a little kasuri methi

No turmeric, no red chili powder — the character is green chili + whole spice. Amounts by feel.

How we make it

  1. Bloom the whole spices. Heat olive oil; add cloves, cinnamon, cardamom and bay leaves for a few seconds.
  2. Sweat the onions. Add the thinly sliced onions and all the salt for the curry. Let them sweat, release their water, then fry until just starting to brown — not fully browned.
  3. Ginger-garlic & chilies. Add the ginger-garlic paste and lots of green chilies. Sauté until the raw smell is gone — you'll know.
  4. Brown the meat. Add the mutton and sauté a few minutes.
  5. Pressure-cook the meat. Add just a little water (3–4 tbsp, so it steams and doesn't catch). Seal and cook on high pressure 30 min.
  6. Make the coarse mix. Meanwhile, in a mortar & pestle crush the coriander seeds, cumin, black peppercorns and a little kasuri methi to a coarse mix.
  7. Add potatoes & dahi. Depressurize. Add the potatoes, half the spice mix, about a cup of yogurt and the mint leaves. Mix so the potatoes settle under the meat and sauce.
  8. Cook the potatoes. Pressure-cook 10 min (or simmer until the potatoes are done). Check the salt.
  9. Finish. Open up, garnish with the rest of the coarse spice mix, stir gently. Serve with rice, roti or naan.
Amma's note: keep it saucy for naan and roti — they soak up the juices. For roti-dry, cook it down a little more. The spice mix goes in twice — half while cooking, half at the end for fragrance.
