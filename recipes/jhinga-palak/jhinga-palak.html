Jhinga Palak

from the kitchen of Noor

A quick weeknight pan · ready in about 20 minutes

What you'll need

  • jumbo shrimp, tails & shells off, cleaned
  • a lot of spinach (palak), chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • ginger-garlic paste, a good spoon
  • olive oil, for frying
  • salt (namak), to taste
  • turmeric (haldi), a pinch
  • red chili powder (mirchi), to taste
  • coriander powder (dhania)

Extra-large / jumbo shrimp hold up best — they stay plump in the quick cook. Amounts by feel; taste as you go.

How we make it

  1. Fry the onions. Heat olive oil and fry the chopped onion until lightly golden brown.
  2. Ginger-garlic. Add the ginger-garlic paste and fry with the onions until the raw smell is gone.
  3. Add the palak. Tip in lots of chopped spinach along with salt, turmeric, chili powder and coriander powder.
  4. Cook it down. Cook and cook until all the water from the spinach is gone and it's wilted and cooked through.
  5. Add the shrimp. Slip in the cleaned jumbo shrimp and cook quickly — about 2 minutes — just until they turn opaque and are done.
  6. Serve. That's it. Lovely with rice or roti.
Amma's note: the shrimp go in last and cook fast — two minutes, no more. Overcooked shrimp turn rubbery, so pull the pan the moment they curl and turn opaque.
