Everyday Loaf

from the kitchen of Yahya

1 standard loaf · Rise 2 hrs · Bake 30–35 min · 375°F (190°C)

The dough that does everything. Built on a baker's formula at 64% hydration — soft enough for sandwiches, sturdy enough to shape free-form. The percentages are by weight of flour, so scale it up or down and it stays true.

Formula · 1 loaf

  • Flour (AP or bread)375 g · 100%
  • Water (warm)240 g · 64%
  • Butter or Oil14 g · 3.7%
  • Sugar (or Honey)12 g · 3.2%
  • Salt9 g · 2.4%
  • Active Dry Yeast7 g · 1.9%
Total Dough657 g · 175.2%

Hydration = water ÷ flour × 100 = 64%. Aim for a final dough temp of 76–78°F (24–26°C) — adjust water temp to hit it.

Method

  1. Bloom the yeast. Stir the yeast (7 g) and sugar (12 g) into the warm water (240 g). Rest 5–10 min until foamy.
  2. Mix. Combine flour (375 g) and salt (9 g). Add the yeast water and butter or oil (14 g). Mix to a rough, shaggy dough.
  3. Knead. 10 min by hand (or 6–7 min in a stand mixer on medium) until smooth and elastic — it should pass a windowpane test.
  4. Bulk rise. Cover and ferment ~1 hr at warm room temperature, until doubled.
  5. Shape. Gently degas, shape into a tight loaf, seam-side down in a greased 9×5 in pan (or free-form on a tray).
  6. Proof. Cover and proof 45–60 min, until the dough crowns ~1 in above the rim and springs back slowly when poked.
  7. Bake. 375°F (190°C) for 30–35 min, until deep golden and the internal temperature reads 200–205°F (93–96°C).
  8. Cool. Turn out and cool on a rack at least 1 hr before slicing — cutting hot bread gums the crumb.
Yahya's note: the loaf is done when the thermometer says so, not when the timer rings. 64% hydration is forgiving — wetter for a more open crumb, drier for tidy sandwich slices, but weigh it either way.
