Everyday Loaf

from the kitchen of Yahya

1 loaf · Rise 2 hrs · Bake 30–35 min · 375°F (190°C)

Formula · 1 loaf

  • Flour (AP or bread)375 g · 100%
  • Sugar (or Honey)12 g · 3.2%
  • Water (warm)240 g · 64%
  • Salt9 g · 2.4%
  • Butter or Oil14 g · 3.7%
  • Active Dry Yeast7 g · 1.9%
Total Dough657 g · 175.2%

Hydration = water ÷ flour × 100 = 64%. Aim for a final dough temp of 76–78°F (24–26°C).

Method

  1. Bloom the yeast. Stir yeast (7 g) and sugar (12 g) into warm water (240 g). Rest 5–10 min until foamy.
  2. Mix. Combine flour (375 g) and salt (9 g). Add the yeast water and butter or oil (14 g). Mix to a rough dough.
  3. Knead. 10 min by hand until smooth and elastic — it should pass a windowpane test.
  4. Bulk rise. Cover and ferment ~1 hr at warm room temperature, until doubled.
  5. Shape. Degas, shape into a tight loaf, seam-side down in a greased 9×5 in pan.
  6. Proof. Cover and proof 45–60 min, until it crowns ~1 in above the rim.
  7. Bake. 375°F (190°C) for 30–35 min, until deep golden and 200–205°F (93–96°C) inside.
  8. Cool. Turn out and cool on a rack at least 1 hr before slicing.
Yahya's note: the loaf is done when the thermometer says so, not when the timer rings. 64% hydration is forgiving — but weigh it either way.
