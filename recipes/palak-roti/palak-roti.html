Palak Roti

from the kitchen of Noor

A big batch · press a week's worth & freeze

What you'll need

For the dough

  • atta (wheat flour), about 1 kg
  • the palak paste (below), ~750 g
  • extra atta / water, as needed to knead

For the palak paste

  • 2 big bunches spinach (palak), washed & cut
  • 10–15 cloves garlic
  • a bunch of green chilies
  • a good bit of cumin (jeera)
  • salt — don't forget it
  • a little water, to help it grind

Amounts are by feel — scale the flour and paste together (roughly 750 g paste to 1 kg atta).

How we make it

  1. Make the paste. Blend the spinach, garlic, green chilies, cumin and salt with just a little water into a fine paste.
  2. Bring the dough together. Put the atta (~1 kg) in a big bowl and add about 750 g of the paste. Mix.
  3. Knead. Knead well (gundo) into a smooth dough ball, adding a little more atta or water as needed for your flour.
  4. Portion. Divide into ~50 g balls.
  5. Press. Press each ball between two parchment sheets (we use a tortilla press) into a roti. Repeat for the batch.
  6. Freeze the extras. Stack with parchment between and freeze — a whole week's worth keeps beautifully.
  7. Cook. Cook fresh or from frozen on a hot tava until cooked through with a few charred spots.
  8. Serve. With curry and ghee, or roll it into a wrap.
Amma's note: we made ours a wrap with fried chicken, raita and fried green beans — delicious. Full of iron and fiber, a little spicy, and healthy either way you eat it.
