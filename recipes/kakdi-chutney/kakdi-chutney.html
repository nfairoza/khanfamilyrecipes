Kakdi Chutney

Tangy, lightly spicy, a little sweet. A cucumber chutney made with slightly raw kakdi — cooked down with tomato, tamarind and green chili, ground coarse with raw garlic, then a handful of raw diced kakdi stirred back in for those bright, crunchy, tangy bites. Wonderful with white rice, a fried protein, or an omelet.

Kakdi Chutney

from the kitchen of Noor

A bowlful · tangy, lightly spicy & a little sweet

What you'll need

  • 3–4 kakdi (cucumber), slightly raw — half green, firm to the touch
  • 2–3 tomatoes
  • green chilies, a few
  • tamarind — a small ball, or a big spoon of concentrate
  • a whole head of raw garlic
  • oil, for the pan
  • cumin seeds (zira)
  • salt (namak), to taste

Pick kakdi that's slightly hard to the touch — never soft. Don't use any that's gone soft. Amounts by feel.

How we make it

  1. Prep the kakdi. Peel and deseed. Dice about a quarter of it very small and set aside raw — these go in at the end for tangy, crunchy bites. Cut the rest into slices or cubes.
  2. Start the pan. Heat oil and let the cumin seeds splutter.
  3. Cook it down. Add the cut kakdi, chopped tomatoes, green chilies and tamarind. Cover and cook until all the vegetables are fully soft.
  4. Cool it. Let the cooked mixture cool down before grinding.
  5. Grind coarse. In a mixer, put the raw garlic, the cooled mixture and salt. Grind just a few seconds — leave it slightly coarse, not a smooth paste.
  6. Stir in the raw kakdi. Tip into a bowl and mix in the reserved raw diced kakdi. Ready to eat.
Amma's note: the raw kakdi stirred in at the end is the whole trick — don't skip it, that's where the bright tangy crunch comes from. Keep the grind coarse, not smooth.
