Munshi Naan

Makes 4 naan · Mix 15 min · Rise 60–90 min · Cook 2–3 min each
Breads
A soft, pillowy yeasted naan enriched with yogurt and ghee. Weighed, not guessed — measured to the gram so it comes out the same every time. The yogurt keeps the crumb tender; the ghee gives it that glossy, restaurant-style finish.

Ingredients · scaled for 4

  • All-Purpose Flour240 g
  • Active Dry Yeast (Khamir)3 g
  • Sugar4 g
  • Warm Milk (or water)120 g
  • Salt4.5 g
  • Plain Yogurt30 g
  • Ghee (or melted butter)14 g
Milk warm, not hot — ~105°F (40°C). Hotter than 115°F (46°C) kills the yeast.

Method

  1. Bloom the yeast. Stir the yeast and sugar into the warm milk (120 g, ~105°F / 40°C). Let it stand 5–10 min until foamy. No foam means dead yeast — start over.
  2. Mix the dough. In a bowl combine the flour (240 g) and salt (4.5 g). Add the yogurt (30 g), ghee (14 g) and the yeast milk. Mix to a shaggy dough.
  3. Knead. Knead 8–10 min by hand (or 5 min in a stand mixer on medium) until smooth, soft and slightly tacky — no less. It should spring back when pressed.
  4. First rise. Cover and rest in a warm spot 60–90 min, until doubled in size.
  5. Divide & shape. Knock back, divide into 4 equal pieces (~102 g each). Ball them, cover, and rest 10 min so the gluten relaxes.
  6. Roll. Roll or stretch each into a teardrop ~8 in long and ~1/4 in thick.
  7. Cook hot. Cook on a screaming-hot cast-iron skillet or tava, 2–3 min total — flip once big bubbles form and the underside is charred in spots.
  8. Finish. Brush with extra ghee straight off the heat. Keep wrapped in a towel until serving so they stay soft.
Yahya's note: weigh everything. The yogurt is not optional — it is what makes the crumb tender instead of bready. If the dough fights the roll, let it rest another 5 min; relaxed gluten rolls thin without tearing.

Everyday Loaf

1 standard loaf · 64% hydration · Mix 20 min · Rise 2 hrs · Bake 30–35 min · Oven 375°F (190°C)
Breads
A versatile, easily handled white loaf built on a baker's formula — 64% hydration, the dough that does everything. Soft enough for sandwiches, sturdy enough to shape free-form. The percentages are by weight of flour, so you can scale it up or down and it stays true.

Formula · 1 loaf

  • Flour (AP or bread)375 g · 100%
  • Water (warm)240 g · 64%
  • Butter or Oil14 g · 3.7%
  • Sugar (or Honey)12 g · 3.2%
  • Salt9 g · 2.4%
  • Active Dry Yeast7 g · 1.9%
Total Dough Mass657 g · 175.2%
Hydration = water ÷ flour × 100 = 64%. Aim for a final dough temp (DDT) of 76–78°F (24–26°C) — adjust water temp to hit it.

Method

  1. Bloom the yeast. Stir the yeast (7 g) and sugar (12 g) into the warm water (240 g). Rest 5–10 min until foamy.
  2. Mix. Combine the flour (375 g) and salt (9 g). Add the yeast water and the butter or oil (14 g). Mix to a rough, shaggy dough.
  3. Knead. Knead 10 min by hand (or 6–7 min in a stand mixer on medium) until smooth and elastic. The dough should pass a windowpane test — stretch thin without tearing.
  4. Bulk rise. Cover and ferment ~1 hr at warm room temperature, until doubled.
  5. Shape. Gently degas, shape into a tight loaf, and place seam-side down in a greased 9×5 in pan (or shape free-form on a tray).
  6. Proof. Cover and proof 45–60 min, until the dough crowns ~1 in above the rim and springs back slowly when poked.
  7. Bake. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 30–35 min, until deep golden and the internal temperature reads 200–205°F (93–96°C).
  8. Cool. Turn out and cool on a rack at least 1 hr before slicing — cutting hot bread gums the crumb.
Yahya's note: the loaf is done when the thermometer says so, not when the timer rings. 64% hydration is forgiving — wetter for a more open crumb, drier for tidy sandwich slices, but weigh it either way.
