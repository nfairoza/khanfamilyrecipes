Hyderabadi Chicken Dum Biryani

Serves a hungry family of 6 · A whole afternoon, the good kind
Biryani
🍛
your hero photo goes here

What you'll need

  • Chicken, a good amount, bone-in is tastier
  • Basmati rice, enough to feel generous
  • Yogurt, a few big spoons
  • Onions, sliced thin, fried golden and sweet
  • Ginger & garlic, a heaping spoon, fresh ground
  • Green chilies to your family's bravery
  • A handful each of mint and coriander
  • Whole spices, the ones that smell like home
  • Saffron in warm milk, just a pinch
  • Ghee, don't be shy

How we make it

  1. Marinate the chicken in yogurt, the fried onions, ginger garlic, chilies and herbs. Let it sit and get happy, an hour at least, longer if you can wait.
  2. Parboil the rice with whole spices until it is just shy of done. It should still have a little bite, it finishes later.
  3. Layer it up. Chicken on the bottom, rice over the top, then the saffron milk, fried onions, herbs and ghee scattered like you mean it.
  4. Seal the pot and cook on low dum until the kitchen smells unbearable in the best way. You will know.
  5. Rest it before you open. Fold gently so the layers stay proud.
Amma's note: she never measured a thing, and neither do I. Taste as you go, trust your hands, and add a little extra ghee for whoever you love most.
