# Voices — how each cook's recipe is written

One book, two voices. Each recipe gets **one** voice and **one** matching signature.
The category sets the color; the cook sets the words. Never mix voices on a page.

## Noor — ancestral / touch-and-feel

Loose, sensory, warm. Cooking as inherited instinct, not lab work.

- **Quantities are feelings:** "a good amount", "a generous pinch", "to your
  family's bravery" (for chili), "add extra ghee for whoever you love most".
- **Doneness is sensory:** "cook until the kitchen smells unbearable in the best
  way", "until the onions give up", "you will know".
- **Convert precise measures to feel** when rendering a recipe in Noor's voice:
  `2 tsp chili powder` → "chili to your family's bravery"; `sauté 8 min` → "until
  golden and sweet-smelling".
- **The note box is "Amma's note"** — a true remembered detail, in her warm voice.
  Don't invent memories; if there isn't one, ask or leave the note out.

Example step: *"Let the onions take their time — stir now and then until they turn
the color of good jaggery. You will know."*

## Yahya — precise

Exact, confident, measured-not-guessed.

- **Quantities are dual:** cups **and** grams — "1½ cups (300 g) basmati".
- **Temperatures dual:** °F (°C). Internal temps stated where relevant — "165°F
  (74°C) at the thickest part".
- **Times explicit**, techniques structured: "in 3 additions", "rest 10 minutes,
  no less".
- **The note box is "Yahya's note"** — a tip about technique or a tested variation.

Example step: *"Bloom the whole spices in 3 tbsp (40 g) ghee at medium heat for
90 seconds — fragrant, not browned."*

## The no-fabricated-precision rule

Rendering a loose recipe in the **measure** voice means inventing numbers. Don't
present estimates as the family's tested measurements: confirm them with the cook,
or mark converted values approximate ("~180°C"). Going the other way
(measure → feel) is always safe.

## Attribution when untagged

Loose, sensory, story-ish notes → probably Noor. Gram weights, temps, timed steps →
probably Yahya. Infer, then confirm in one line before building. A cook can override
per recipe (Noor writing a precise bake → use the measure voice for that page, but
keep her signature).

## Attribution & headnote

The cook's name sits directly under the title: "from the kitchen of Noor" in the
Caveat script, accent color — like a hand-signed byline. Light, not loud.

The **headnote** (2–3 italic sentences above the hero) is the story: a real memory
in the cook's voice, lead-in words bold accent. Ask for it or pull it from what
Noor has actually said — never invent a memory. No memory, no headnote; the page
works without it.

The **original card scan** is the deepest heirloom layer: whenever a photo/scan of
the handwritten original exists, offer to tape it in with a Caveat caption
("Amma's original card, in her own hand").
